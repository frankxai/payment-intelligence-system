## PSD3 (Payment Services Directive 3)

**Status as of 2026-06:** In force (adopted EU, member state implementation ongoing).

**What changed from PSD2:**
- Expanded strong customer authentication (SCA) requirements, with specific provisions for agent-initiated payments
- Operator accountability for AI agents initiating payments: the PSP of the account holder is responsible for ensuring the agent was properly authorized
- "Know-Your-Payer" provisions: PSPs must be able to trace agent-initiated transactions back to an identified human account holder or legal entity operator
- Stronger data access rules for third-party payment service providers

**Agentic payment implications:**
- An AI agent making payments on behalf of a human is a "payment initiation service" under PSD3
- Operators must ensure the agent's authorization is documented and revocable
- SCA may be required at mandate creation time (when the human grants the agent authorization), even if individual agent transactions do not require additional authentication

**Counsel routing:** PSD3 implementation varies by member state. For specific instrument compliance, engage an EU-licensed payment law firm in the relevant member state.

## MiCA (Markets in Crypto-Assets Regulation)

**Status as of 2026-06:** In force (all provisions applicable as of end-2024).

**What it covers:**
- Stablecoins (asset-referenced tokens, e-money tokens) issued or traded in the EU
- Crypto-asset service providers (CASPs) operating in the EU
- White-paper requirements for significant stablecoins

**Agentic payment implications:**
- If your agent uses stablecoin rails (USDC, USDT) for settlement, and the stablecoin is issued by an EU entity or traded on an EU platform, MiCA CASP licensing requirements may apply to the intermediary
- Using USDC (Circle) for EU-user agent payments requires Circle's MiCA compliance to be verified — Circle holds EU e-money token authorization as of 2026-06
- Agents operating as "crypto-asset service providers" in the EU may need CASP registration

**Counsel routing:** MiCA compliance for specific stablecoin rails and CASP activities requires EU crypto law counsel.

## EU AI Act

**Status as of 2026-06:** In force (phased implementation; high-risk AI provisions applicable).

**What it covers for payment agents:**
- AI systems used in financial services that affect individuals' access to credit, insurance, or payment services may be classified as "high-risk"
- High-risk AI systems require: risk management system, technical documentation, logging of AI decisions, human oversight capability, accuracy/robustness requirements, transparency to users

**Agentic payment implications:**
- An AI agent that initiates payments on behalf of individuals may be in-scope as a high-risk AI system depending on the operator's assessment
- Operators of in-scope agents must maintain logs of agent decisions, including payment authorization decisions
- "Human oversight" requirement: the operator must be able to override or halt the agent's payment activity
- KYA credential architecture satisfies the traceability requirement if properly implemented

**Counsel routing:** AI Act classification (high-risk vs. general-purpose) requires legal counsel with AI Act expertise. Classification is operator's responsibility, not PSP's.

## VAT and Sales Tax (EU)

**Status as of 2026-06:** EU VAT rules apply to digital services; OSS (One-Stop Shop) scheme in force.

**Agentic payment implications:**
- If your agent buys digital services from EU merchants, VAT will typically be charged at the destination rate
- If your business sells services to EU customers via agents, VAT registration (or OSS registration) is required if turnover exceeds EU thresholds
- Agent transaction records must capture VAT amounts and merchant VAT ID for reclaim

**Counsel routing:** VAT compliance for cross-border digital services requires accountant familiar with EU VAT OSS scheme in your jurisdiction.
